Bringing AEDs
in New York Online

Our mission is to bring all geographic location records for Automatic External Defibrillators (AEDs) in the State of New York online and make them accessible to anyone in need. Currently, the survival rate of an out-of-hospital cardiac arrest is under 12%. We hope to help increase this rate by providing some much-needed information.

Who are we?

The New York State Chapter of the American College of Cardiology (NYACC) was founded in 1990, but our history in New York is much older. In fact the American College of Cardiology (ACC) was started by a group of physicians from New York. The Chapter was formed for the purpose of contributing to the prevention of cardiovascular diseases and ensuring optimal quality of care for individuals with such diseases.

What is an AED?

An Automatic External Defibrillator is a portable electronic device that automatically diagnoses the life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias, and is able to treat them through the application of electricity which allows the heart to re-establish an effective rhythm.
